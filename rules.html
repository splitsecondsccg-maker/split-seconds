Split Seconds - Official Rulebook

The Premise

Split Seconds is a high-speed, double-blind dueling game where combat happens in flashes of precognition. You and your opponent secretly plan a sequence of strikes, defenses, abilities, Concentrations, and tricks across a 5-Moment Timeline. Then one moment is revealed in the Flash, and both players may decide whether to hold firm or Pivot.

The Objective: Reduce your opponent's Health Points (HP) to 0 before they do the same to you.

1. Core Resources, Proficiencies & Decks

  • Health (HP): Your life force. If it reaches 0, you lose.
  • Stamina: The energy used to play cards, Parry, and Pivot. Stronger actions cost more Stamina.
  • Armor: Reduces incoming damage. Some passives and cards temporarily increase effective Armor.
  • Timeline: Each round has 5 moments. You place actions into those moments. Some actions resolve only on their final moment; others resolve on every moment they occupy.
  • Character Passive: Every character has a unique passive effect that is always active during the match.
  • Proficiencies: Cards require one or more proficiencies, such as Warrior, Assassin, Sorcerer, Wizard, Blood, Darkness, Light, Poison, Ice, Hypnotic, and more. Your character can only use cards whose requirements they satisfy.
  • Hand Limit: You may hold at most 7 cards. If a draw would exceed 7, extra cards are lost.
  • Decks: Matches use constructed decks. The current base decks are 24 cards, with up to 3 copies of the same card.

2. Action Types

The core triangle is still the heart of combat: Attacks beat Grabs, Grabs beat Concentrations/defensive moments, and Defenses beat Attacks. But the modern rules also distinguish Utility, Enhancers, and Multi-Active actions.

  • Attack: Deals damage. If not stopped, it hits the opponent. Attacks beat Grabs because they interrupt the opponent before the Grab can succeed.
  • Block (basic action, 0 Stamina): Reduces incoming damage in that moment. Blocks do not stop the attack entirely; they just absorb damage.
  • Parry (basic action, 1 Stamina): Hard-counters an Attack in the same moment. A successful Parry negates the attack entirely and gives the attacker the Parry draw penalty next round.
  • Grab: Punishes Block and Concentration. Grabs bypass Block and Parry. If the opposing slot contains an Attack, Utility, or nothing relevant to Grab, the Grab misses.
  • Utility: Technical actions that are not Attacks, Grabs, or Concentrations. Utility is not interrupted by Grab unless a card explicitly says otherwise.
  • Concentration (icon: brain): Setup, ritual, stance, or ongoing focus card type. A normal Concentration is interrupted entirely by a successful Grab in the same moment.
  • Enhancer (icon: sparkle): A card that attaches to another action instead of taking a timeline slot itself. Every Enhancer must list which action types it can legally attach to.
  • Empty Slot: Leaving a slot empty is legal and often useful. It can make the opponent's Grab miss.

2.1 Concentration vs Utility

  • Grab interrupts Concentration.
  • Grab does not interrupt Utility by default.
  • If you are unsure, trust the card type shown on the card or tooltip.

2.2 Enhancers

  • Enhancers are dragged onto an existing legal action during Planning.
  • They do not occupy timeline slots by themselves.
  • If the parent action returns to hand, attached Enhancers return too, as separate cards.
  • If the parent action is fully canceled, its Enhancers are lost with it unless a card effect says otherwise.
  • Some Enhancers are tempo pieces, some are value pieces, and some are payoff pieces. Many weaker Enhancers replace themselves on on_first_resolve so they are not dead cards.

2.3 Multi-Active Actions

Some actions use a distinct special color because they are Multi-Active: they resolve on each moment they occupy, not only once at the end.

  • Multi-Active Attack: each occupied moment is its own damage window.
  • Multi-Active Concentration: each occupied moment is its own resolve window.
  • Grab and Parry interact moment-by-moment with Multi-Active actions, not with the whole card at once.
  • Enhancers attached to a Multi-Active action can matter on multiple moments. This is intentional.

Example: Multi-Active Concentration

You place a Multi-Active Concentration on Moments 2-4. The opponent Grabs on Moment 3.

Result: Moment 3 is interrupted, but Moments 2 and 4 still resolve.

Example: Multi-Active Attack + Enhancer

You attach an Enhancer that adds damage or an on_hit effect to a Multi-Active Attack.

Result: that attached effect can apply on multiple occupied moments if the attack keeps resolving. This is part of the card's intended power.

3. Statuses & Live Conditions

Statuses are tracked under the fighter portraits. Many cards are conditional: they become stronger, cheaper, or gain extra effects when a state is active.

  • FREEZE: Stackable. Persists between turns. At 10+ FREEZE, Attacks cost +1 Stamina until FREEZE is reduced.
  • BLEED: Stackable. Persists until consumed or removed. Many Blood cards scale with BLEED or cash it out for burst damage.
  • POISON: Stackable. Deals damage at end of turn and then partially decays.
  • HYPNOTIZED: Non-stackable. It is not removed at the start of turn. It is removed when that fighter loses life. Some cards consume it for stronger effects.
  • EXHAUSTED: Non-stackable. Reduces the next stamina regeneration, then clears.
  • Bonus Armor / Armor Debuff / Draw Less / Next Attack Mod / Next Grab Mod: temporary hidden combat values created by abilities, passives, and card effects.

Live cues

If a card is currently enabled by a condition, the game gives it a live glow. Hovering timeline cards also shows current live changes such as:

  • cost changing from a passive
  • damage being increased
  • added effects from passives or Enhancers
  • triggered text such as on_first_resolve, on_first_hit, and on_hit

4. Round Structure

Each round follows the same sequence.

Phase 1: Regenerate, Exert, Draw

  1. Regenerate: Both players recover 3 Stamina up to max, modified by effects like EXHAUSTED.
  2. Exert: You may discard cards from hand for +1 Stamina each.
  3. Draw: Both players draw up to 2 cards, modified by draw penalties such as Parry penalty or draw-less effects.

Phase 2: Planning

Both players secretly place actions on their 5-moment timeline. Costs are paid up front. Enhancers are attached to actions during this phase.

Phase 3: Flash

One random moment is revealed. Each player sees only what was in that flashed moment.

Phase 4: Pivot

  • You may pay a 1 Stamina Pivot tax to cancel the exact flashed action you revealed.
  • The canceled action returns to hand and its original card cost is refunded.
  • You then refill those freed moments in a mini-planning window.
  • Important: the Flash reveals only what was there at that moment. It does not reveal what either player pivots into afterward.
  • If you have 0 Stamina left after Planning, you cannot Pivot.

Phase 5: Resolution

The timeline resolves from Moment 1 to Moment 5.

5. Same-Moment Timing Priority

  • Step 1 - Hard interruption / negation: Parry, Grab interruption, and special cancel effects are checked first.
  • Step 2 - Damage: surviving Attacks and Grabs deal damage. Attack-vs-Attack is simultaneous.
  • Step 3 - Triggered text: on_hit, on_first_hit, blocked/parried text, defender on_block / on_parry, then on_resolve / on_first_resolve.
  • Step 4 - Expire text: once an action has finished its full duration, on_expire resolves.
  • Step 5 - Legacy/special handlers: older effect keys and card-specific scripting resolve after the main trigger system.

Trigger meanings

  • on_hit: every successful hit by that action.
  • on_first_hit: only the first successful hit by that action.
  • on_resolve: every time the action resolves.
  • on_first_resolve: only the first time the action resolves.
  • on_expire: when the full action ends, even if it never hit.

Example: Draw enhancer wording

If an Enhancer says On first resolve: draw 1, that draw happens the first time the parent action starts resolving.

If it instead says On first hit: draw 1, the action must actually connect first.

6. Important Interaction Rules

  • Attack into Grab: Attack wins. The Grab misses.
  • Grab into Block: Grab connects and bypasses Block.
  • Grab into Parry: Grab connects and bypasses Parry.
  • Grab into Concentration: a normal Concentration is negated entirely.
  • Grab into Multi-Active Concentration: only that moment is interrupted.
  • Grab into Utility: Grab misses by default.
  • Parry into Multi-Active Attack: Parry only stops the contested moment, not the whole action.
  • If both attacks resolve in the same moment: they hit simultaneously. "Next attack" bonuses affect future attacks, not that simultaneous exchange.

7. Tactical Notes

Advanced Tips

Save Pivot stamina: ending Planning on 0 Stamina means you cannot Pivot, even if the Flash shows disaster.

Empty slots matter: leaving a moment empty is sometimes the best anti-Grab play.

Think in windows: many cards care about thresholds like 3+ BLEED, 5+ FREEZE, 10+ BLEED, or empty enemy hand. Build toward those windows, then cash them out.

Enhancers change timing math: on Multi-Active actions, attached damage and on_hit text can snowball across several moments.
